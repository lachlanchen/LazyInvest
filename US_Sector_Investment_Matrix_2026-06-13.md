## Bucket Definitions

| Bucket | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Best now | Sectors or companies with strong current fundamentals, visible catalysts, and a better balance of quality versus risk. These can still be expensive. |
| Hot and still good | Widely followed winners where fundamentals remain strong, but valuation and expectations are already high. |
| Less noticed and good | Smaller or quieter companies with credible growth, improving economics, or niche leadership. These need more due diligence. |
| Avoid / very unattractive | Companies where the current evidence shows weak unit economics, heavy losses, cash burn, high leverage, dilution, no near-term revenue engine, or broken business model risk. |
| Less known, huge risk | Speculative companies with very large upside if execution works, but binary technical, regulatory, financing, or adoption risk. Position sizing would matter more than thesis excitement. |

## Sector Notes

### 1. AI Compute, Semiconductors, Cloud, Networking

Current view: Attractive but crowded. The best "less obvious" ideas may be suppliers that sell into AI infrastructure rather than only the front-page GPU winners.

Hot and still good:

- `NVDA`: Still the core AI compute winner. NVIDIA reported Q1 FY2027 revenue of $81.6B, up 85% year over year, with Data Center revenue of $75.2B, up 92%. That is extraordinary scale growth. The risk is expectations, customer concentration, export restrictions, and hyperscaler capex cyclicality. Source: [NVIDIA Q1 FY2027 results](https://investor.nvidia.com/news/press-release-details/2026/NVIDIA-Announces-Financial-Results-for-First-Quarter-Fiscal-2027/default.aspx)
- `AVGO`: Stronger than a simple chip-stock story because AI custom accelerators, networking, and infrastructure software all matter. Broadcom reported Q2 FY2026 revenue of $22.187B, up 48%; AI semiconductor revenue was $10.8B, up 143%. Source: [Broadcom Q2 FY2026 results](https://investors.broadcom.com/news-releases/news-release-details/broadcom-inc-announces-second-quarter-fiscal-year-2026-financial)
- `AMD`: A credible challenger in AI and data-center compute. Q1 2026 revenue was $10.3B, gross margin 53%, and non-GAAP EPS $1.37. Source: [AMD Q1 2026 results](https://ir.amd.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/1284/amd-reports-first-quarter-2026-financial-results)
- `AMZN`: AWS is a major AI infrastructure beneficiary. Amazon Q1 2026 net sales were $181.5B, up 17%, and AWS operating income was $14.2B versus $11.5B a year earlier. Source: [Amazon Q1 2026 results](https://ir.aboutamazon.com/news-release/news-release-details/2026/Amazon-com-Announces-First-Quarter-Results/default.aspx)

Less noticed and good:

- `BELFB`: Electronic components, power, connectivity, defense, and data infrastructure exposure. Bel Fuse Q1 2026 sales rose 17.2%; management cited networking/data infrastructure and AI-driven high-performance compute demand. The May 2026 Class B offering can help fund debt repayment and acquisition plans, but adds share-count dilution and use-of-proceeds monitoring. Bel also declared regular quarterly cash dividends on May 21, 2026, including $0.07 per Class B share payable July 31, a small cash-return signal rather than a thesis driver. Sources: [Bel Fuse Q1 2026 results](https://ir.belfuse.com/node/18386/pdf), [Bel Fuse Class B offering](https://ir.belfuse.com/news-releases/news-release-details/bel-fuse-inc-announces-pricing-upsized-public-offering-class-b), [Bel Fuse dividend](https://ir.belfuse.com/news-releases/news-release-details/bel-fuse-inc-announces-regular-quarterly-cash-dividend-its-71)

Speculative:

- `IONQ`: Real revenue acceleration but still heavy adjusted EBITDA losses. Q1 2026 revenue was $64.7M, up 755%, with full-year guidance raised to $260M-$270M; adjusted EBITDA loss was $(96.8)M. Source: [IonQ Q1 2026 results](https://www.ionq.com/news/ionq-announces-first-quarter-2026-financial-results)
- `RGTI`: Smaller quantum peer. Q1 2026 revenue was reported at $4.4M and operating loss around $26M in public summaries. This is high technical and financing risk. Source: [Rigetti Q1 2026 summary](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/rigetti-computing-reports-first-quarter-200500873.html)

What to monitor:

- Hyperscaler capex guidance.
- AI networking and custom accelerator revenue.
- Export restrictions and China exposure.
- Gross margin stability.
- Customer concentration in AI hardware.

### 2. Power, Grid, Cooling, and Data-Center Infrastructure

Current view: One of the best themes now. AI is not just chips. It needs electricity, cooling, switchgear, environmental systems, and energy efficiency.

Why the sector matters:

- The EIA expects data-center server electricity use to grow across the U.S. commercial building stock, with standalone data centers growing fastest. Source: [EIA data-center electricity use](https://www.eia.gov/todayinenergy/detail.php?id=67704)
- The IEA estimated global data-center electricity consumption around 415 TWh in 2024 and projects strong growth through 2030. Source: [IEA Energy and AI](https://www.iea.org/reports/energy-and-ai/energy-demand-from-ai)

Hot and still good:

- `VRT`: Critical digital infrastructure, power, and cooling. Vertiv Q1 2026 net sales were $2.65B, up 30%, with 23% organic sales growth, and management raised full-year guidance. Source: [Vertiv Q1 2026 results](https://investors.vertiv.com/news/news-details/2026/Vertiv-Reports-Strong-First-Quarter-with-Diluted-EPS-Growth-of-136-Adjusted-Diluted-EPS-Growth-of-83-Raises-Full-Year-Guidance/default.aspx)
- `CEG`: Nuclear power is increasingly valuable for data-center baseload power. Constellation signed a 20-year PPA with Microsoft tied to the restart of Three Mile Island Unit 1 as the Crane Clean Energy Center. Source: [Constellation Microsoft PPA](https://www.constellationenergy.com/news/2024/Constellation-to-Launch-Crane-Clean-Energy-Center-Restoring-Jobs-and-Carbon-Free-Power-to-The-Grid.html)

Less noticed and good:

- `CECO`: Industrial air, water, emissions, and power/thermal infrastructure. Q1 2026 orders were $449.5M, up 97%; backlog was $1.035B, up 72%. CECO closed the Thermon acquisition on June 1, 2026, and the June 9 combined-company outlook calls for 2026 revenue of $1.275B-$1.375B, non-GAAP adjusted EBITDA of $195M-$225M, and free cash flow of at least 55% of adjusted EBITDA. Sources: [CECO Q1 2026 results](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/28/3282406/0/en/ceco-environmental-reports-first-quarter-2026-results.html), [CECO Thermon post-close update](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/09/3308707/0/en/ceco-environmental-provides-post-closing-update-on-thermon-transaction.html)
- `WLDN`: Energy efficiency and infrastructure services. FY2026 targets include $410M-$425M net revenue and $100M-$105M adjusted EBITDA. The Burton acquisition adds commercial energy/procurement capability and was described as accretive to 2026 adjusted EPS. A June 18, 2026 Form 8-K disclosed annual-meeting results and stockholder approval of an amended 2008 Performance Incentive Plan adding 380,000 shares to the award reserve; this is not an operating-update change, but it adds an equity-plan dilution and stock-based-compensation monitor. Sources: [Willdan Q1 2026 results](https://ir.willdangroup.com/news-releases/news-release-details/willdan-group-reports-first-quarter-results-2), [Willdan Burton acquisition](https://www.willdan.com/newsdetail?NewsID=willdan-expands-commercial-energy-capabilities-with-acquisition-of-burton-energy-group), [Willdan June 18, 2026 Form 8-K](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1370450/000110465926075795/wldn-20260617x8k.htm)
- `BELFB`: Component exposure to datacenter, defense, and high-performance compute, with the May 2026 Class B offering still an explicit dilution/use-of-proceeds item to monitor and the May 21 regular dividend a modest cash-return signal. Sources: [Bel Fuse Q1 2026 results](https://ir.belfuse.com/node/18386/pdf), [Bel Fuse Class B offering](https://ir.belfuse.com/news-releases/news-release-details/bel-fuse-inc-announces-pricing-upsized-public-offering-class-b), [Bel Fuse dividend](https://ir.belfuse.com/news-releases/news-release-details/bel-fuse-inc-announces-regular-quarterly-cash-dividend-its-71)

Avoid / very unattractive:

- `PLUG`: Improving, but still not investable for conservative capital in my view. Q1 2026 revenue rose 22%, but gross margin remained negative at (13%), operating loss was $(109.5)M, and net loss attributable to Plug was $(245.3)M. Source: [Plug Power Q1 2026 results](https://www.ir.plugpower.com/press-releases/news-details/2026/Plug-Power-Reports-Strong-Q1-2026-Results-with-22-Revenue-Growth-and-71-Margin-Improvement-Year-over-Year/default.aspx)
- `FCEL`: Q2 FY2026 revenue declined 5% to $35.6M; net loss was $(77.6)M; the company used ATM equity sales to raise cash. Source: [FuelCell Energy Q2 FY2026 results](https://investor.fce.com/press-releases/press-release-details/2026/FuelCell-Energy-Reports-Second-Fiscal-Quarter-2026-Results-Advances-Data-Center-Power-Strategy/default.aspx)
- `CHPT`: Q1 FY2027 revenue grew only 4% to $102M, while GAAP net loss was $(43.2)M and cash was $95.8M. Source: [ChargePoint Q1 FY2027 results](https://www.chargepoint.com/about/news/chargepoint-reports-first-quarter-fiscal-year-2027-financial-results)

Speculative:

- `OKLO`, `SMR`, `NNE`: Advanced nuclear can be huge, but many companies are pre-commercial or depend on licensing, fuel availability, project finance, and long construction timelines. Treat as venture-style public equities, not normal utilities.

What to monitor:

- Data-center power interconnection queues.
- Utility capex plans.
- Backlog quality, not just backlog size.
- Gross margin on backlog conversion.
- CECO/Thermon integration, synergy realization, and acquisition-related leverage.
- Willdan equity-plan dilution, stock-based compensation, and Burton integration.
- Nuclear restart/regulatory timelines.

### 3. Cybersecurity, Identity, and Data Protection

Current view: Strong. AI increases both attack capability and enterprise security spend. Prefer companies with recurring revenue, high free cash flow, and platform consolidation.

Hot and still good:

- `CRWD`: Q1 FY2027 net new ARR was $256M, up 32%; operating cash flow was $591M and free cash flow was $468M. Source: [CrowdStrike Q1 FY2027 results](https://ir.crowdstrike.com/news-releases/news-release-details/crowdstrike-reports-first-quarter-fiscal-year-2027-financial)
- `PANW`: Fiscal Q3 2026 revenue grew 31% to $3.0B, Next-Generation Security ARR grew 60% to $8.1B, and remaining performance obligation grew 36% to $18.4B. The caveat is acquisition contribution and GAAP losses in the quarter. Source: [Palo Alto Networks Q3 FY2026 results](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/palo-alto-networks-reports-fiscal-third-quarter-2026-financial-results-302789148.html)
- `NET`: Q1 2026 revenue was $639.8M, up 34%, but it still reported a GAAP operating loss. Great platform, valuation-sensitive. Source: [Cloudflare Q1 2026 results](https://cloudflare.net/news/news-details/2026/Cloudflare-Announces-First-Quarter-2026-Financial-Results/default.aspx)

Less noticed and good:

- `YOU`: Identity platform with strong cash generation. Q1 2026 revenue was $253.0M, up 19.7%; bookings were up 40.8%; free cash flow was $185.5M; members reached 41.0M. Post-Q1 Expedia, Samsung Wallet, GDIT, MIA Concierge, and Indianapolis airport updates support the broader digital-identity thesis beyond airport lanes. Sources: [Clear Secure Q1 2026 results](https://ir.clearme.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/184/clear-announces-first-quarter-2026-financial-results), [CLEAR Expedia partnership](https://ir.clearme.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/186/clear-partners-with-expedia-to-bring-a-more-seamless), [CLEAR Samsung Wallet partnership](https://ir.clearme.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/188/clear-partners-with-samsung-bringing-clear-id-to-samsung), [CLEAR-GDIT collaboration](https://ir.clearme.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/187/clear-and-gdit-announce-strategic-collaboration-agreement), [CLEAR MIA Concierge launch](https://ir.clearme.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/189/concierge-powered-by-clear-launched-at-mia-to-elevate), [CLEAR Indianapolis launch](https://ir.clearme.com/news-events/press-releases/detail/190/clear-launches-at-indianapolis-international-airport)

Avoid / very unattractive:

- I do not have a strong named avoid from the current cybersecurity evidence pass. The avoid rule for this sector is simple: avoid firms with slowing ARR, rising stock-based compensation, weak retention, and no path to cash generation. Valuation alone can also turn a good company into a bad investment.

What to monitor:

- Net new ARR.
- Free cash flow margin.
- Net retention.
- Platform consolidation wins.
- AI security product adoption.
- Breach or outage history.

## Research Rules for Maintaining This Table

Update cadence:

- Refresh monthly, and immediately after earnings for any listed ticker.
- Refresh faster if the company issues new guidance, raises capital, changes auditors, receives FDA/regulatory news, announces a major acquisition, or reports a safety/cyber incident.

Minimum data to update:

- Latest revenue growth.
- Organic versus acquired growth.
- GAAP operating income or loss.
- Free cash flow.
- Cash and debt.
- Dilution and stock-based compensation.
- Backlog, ARR, RPO, or customer count where relevant.
- Next catalyst date.
- Valuation multiple after price changes.

Upgrade signals:

- Revenue growth stays above 20% while margins improve.
- Cash flow improves faster than adjusted EBITDA.
- Backlog converts to revenue without margin collapse.
- Customer concentration decreases.
- Guidance is raised for both revenue and profit/cash flow.

Downgrade signals:

- Revenue growth is mostly acquisition-driven.
- Gross margin deteriorates without a temporary explanation.
- GAAP losses widen while management emphasizes adjusted metrics.
- Cash burn rises and capital raises dilute shareholders.
- A single product, customer, launch, or regulatory event carries most of the thesis.
